Vaccinations

  • Core Vaccines:
    • Canine Distemper (CDV): Usually part of a combined vaccine (DHPP) given in puppyhood with boosters every 1-3 years.
    • Canine Parvovirus (CPV): Also part of the DHPP combination, with regular boosters.
    • Canine Adenovirus (CAV-2): Another component of the DHPP vaccine, for protection against hepatitis.
    • Rabies: Typically given once a year or every three years depending on the vaccine used.
  • Non-Core Vaccines:
    • Leptospirosis: Often recommended yearly, especially if the dog is in an area where this disease is prevalent.
    • Lyme Disease: Recommended if the dog is at risk of tick exposure, generally given annually.
    • Bordetella (Kennel Cough): Given yearly, especially if the dog frequently stays in kennels or around other dogs.

Veterinarian Visits

  • Routine Check-ups:
    • Annual Wellness Exam: General health check, including weight, teeth, coat, and overall condition.
    • Vaccination Boosters: Regular visits for updating core and non-core vaccines.
    • Flea and Tick Prevention: Consultation and application of preventative treatments.
  • Minor Health Issues:
    • Ear Cleaning: Regular ear cleaning to prevent infections, especially if the dog has floppy ears prone to wax buildup.
    • Nail Trimming: Professional trimming to avoid overgrown nails, which can be uncomfortable.
    • Allergy Management: Occasional visits for mild skin allergies or sensitivities, often treated with topical ointments or antihistamines.
  • Grooming-Related:
    • Anal Gland Expression: Routine procedure to prevent discomfort or infection, usually done during grooming visits.
    • Dental Cleaning: Regular teeth cleaning to prevent plaque buildup and maintain oral health.
  • Diet and Weight Management:
    • Dietary Consultations: Visits to discuss optimal diet and weight management for maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
    • Weight Check: Routine weigh-ins to ensure the dog maintains a healthy weight, especially after any dietary changes.
